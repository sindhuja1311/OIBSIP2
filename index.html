Katy Perry is a popular American singer, songwriter, and television judge known for her catchy pop music and vibrant personality known for participating in many UNICEF campaigns as well. Born on October 25, 1984, in Santa Barbara, California, Perry began her career as a gospel singer before transitioning into mainstream pop music. She gained worldwide fame with her breakthrough single "I Kissed a Girl" in 2008, followed by a string of chart-topping hits such as "Teenage Dream," "California Gurls," and "Roar."
Known for her extravagant costumes and elaborate stage productions, Perry has won numerous awards, including four Guinness World Records and five American Music Awards. Her 'Teenage Dream' album hit 8 no.1 singles on billboard charts tying up with legendary Micheal Jackson.
She has sold millions of albums and singles worldwide and is recognized for her powerful vocals and infectious energy. Aside from her music career, Perry has also served as a judge on the singing competition show "American Idol." Her contributions to the music industry and her captivating performances have solidified her status as one of the most successful and influential pop stars of her generation.
